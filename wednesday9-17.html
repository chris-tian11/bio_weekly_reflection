Dehydration Synthesis and Hydrolysis

(Notes on video)

  • Many important biological molecules are made of repeating subunites, called monomers
  • Many monomers create a polymer
    • Ex: Many amino acid monomers create a protein polymer
  • Polymers are formed by using dehydration synthesis reactions
    • Ex: In a dehydration synthesis reaction, the hydrogen from one monomers and the hydroxyl from the other monomer combine to form water
    • The bond links the two monormers together
  • Hydrolysis is the opposite of dehydration synthesis
    • Hydrolysis is when polymers split into monomers
    • Hydro = "Water"
    • Lysis = "Break"
    • The hydroxyl piece of a water molecule goes to one monomer and the hydrogen piece goes to another
